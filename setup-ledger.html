1
Prepare Your Ledger

Before connecting to BlackRoad:

  • Ensure your Ledger is initialized with a PIN
  • Write down your 24-word recovery phrase
  • Install the Bitcoin, Ethereum, or Solana app via Ledger Live
  • Update to the latest firmware version

2
Connect via USB-C

Physical connection:

  • Connect your Ledger to your computer using USB-C cable
  • Enter your PIN on the Ledger device
  • Open the Bitcoin/Ethereum/Solana app on your Ledger
  • Ensure the device shows "Application is ready"

3
Enable Browser Support

Configure your Ledger for web access:

  • Open the Settings on your Ledger device
  • Navigate to "Browser support"
  • Set it to "Yes" or "Enabled"
  • Navigate to "Contract data" and enable it (for Ethereum)

4
Grant WebUSB Permission

When you click "Connect Ledger" in BlackRoad:

  • Browser will show a device selection popup
  • Select your Ledger device (e.g., "Nano S Plus")
  • Click "Connect"
  • Browser will request USB permissions - click "Allow"
// The app will detect your Ledger with vendor ID: 0x2c97 // No drivers needed - pure WebUSB

5
Verify Connection

Once connected, you should see:

  • Green "Connected" status indicator
  • Device information (model, serial, firmware)
  • Your crypto accounts (BTC, ETH, SOL)
  • Current balances

6
Use Ledger Features

Available operations:

  • Get Address: Retrieve your crypto address
  • Sign Transaction: Securely sign outgoing transactions
  • Get Public Key: Export your public key
  • Verify Address: Confirm address on Ledger screen

7
Troubleshooting

If connection fails:

  • Ensure you're using Chrome or Edge (not Firefox or Safari)
  • Try a different USB port or cable
  • Restart your Ledger device
  • Close Ledger Live if it's running
  • Clear browser cache and reload
  • Check that the correct app is open on Ledger
